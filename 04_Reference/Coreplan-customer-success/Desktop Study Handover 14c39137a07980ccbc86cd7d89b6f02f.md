# Desktop Study Criteria

Before conducting the desktop study, it has to meet all the criteria: 

- [ ]  The SPICED framework needs to be completed
- [ ]  At least 2 demos conducted with the decision maker included in them
- [ ]  Ballpark pricing confirmed and they will be on the Enterprise plan
- [ ]  If there is a specialised technical requirement, technical session should have been scheduled to discuss the feasibility
- [ ]  Success criteria determined for the desktop study
- [ ]  Desktop Study handover document should be completed and handed over to Pre-sales CS team for configuration

# What do you need to do before running a desktop study

### Work closely with champion

| Understand the current process | Collaborate with the champion to thoroughly document their existing workflow, including tools, challenges, and areas for improvement. Ensure you can pinpoint specific challenges in their current process and quantify their impact on the business (e.g., time wasted, lost revenue, errors). |
| --- | --- |
| Define success criteria | Determine clear, measurable objectives for the desktop study with the champion. Ask questions like: What are we hoping to achieve with this study? How will this study demonstrate value beyond a standard demo? What specific outcomes will indicate a successful session and justify next steps? |
| Identify risks | Proactively identify potential obstacles with the champion. Are there functionality gaps in CorePlan? Is the budget already available for them to buy? Is there anything that will stagnate this deal (and eventually lose it?) |
| Identify blockers  | Work with the champion to identify who the blockers are. Who are the stakeholders resistant to this change? How much impact do they have on business decisions? And how can we get them over the line? |

### Workshop your findings and dry-run with the champion

Collaborate closely with the champion to workshop, review and refine your findings. You can use a powerpoint to facilitate real-time editing and ensure alignment.  

This presentation should clearly articulate: 

1. The current situation
2. Impact on the business
3. Underlying pain points caused by inefficiencies
4. Map of the current process 
5. Highlight the critical event statement 
6. The envisioned future state with CorePlan 

Encourage the champion to actively participate by providing their insights and filling in any gaps within the presentation.

### Go through the desktop study flow with internal stakeholders

Before conducting the desktop study or the dry run, hold an internal preparation session with key internal stakeholder(s) involved (ie pre-sales CS, and anyone else who will be in the desktop study). 

During the session, review the planned flow of the desktop study, emphasising any key points to highlight and address, including potential gaps in CorePlan‚Äôs capabilities. 

Collaboratively identify potential risks, such as feature limitations or integration challenges, and brainstorm effective solutions to mitigate them. 

This collaborative preparation ensures a smooth and successful desktop study that accurately represents CorePlan's value proposition while addressing any customer concerns proactively.
