* **3\. Our Customer Success Processes**  
  * To ensure consistency, efficiency, and a world-class customer experience, we've established the following key processes:  
    * **3.1. Sales to CS Handoff**  
      * *Objective:* To ensure a seamless transition of new customers from the Sales team to the Customer Success team. This process is critical for setting the stage for a successful implementation and long-term partnership.  
      * *Key Elements:*  
        * **Clear Ownership Transfer**: A well-defined point of transfer of ownership from the Sales representative to the designated Customer Success Manager (CSM).  
        * **Comprehensive Information Sharing**: The Sales team must provide the CSM with all relevant customer information, including:  
          * Customer background, business goals, and key stakeholders.  
          * Detailed information gathered during the sales process (e.g., SPICED framework, pain points, desired outcomes).  
          * Contractual details, including scope of work, agreed-upon deliverables, and timelines.  
          * Any specific customer requirements, technical specifications, or potential challenges.  
        * **Handover Meeting**: A formal meeting between the Sales team and the CSM to review the customer's background, needs, and expectations. This meeting provides an opportunity for the CSM to ask questions, clarify any ambiguities, and begin building a relationship with the customer.  
        * **Documentation**: All relevant customer information, including the agreed-upon scope of work, and gap analysis, must be documented in our CRM system (e.g., Salesforce) to ensure a single source of truth and easy access for all stakeholders.  
        * **Alignment on Next Steps**: A clear agreement on the next steps in the onboarding process, including the implementation kickoff meeting and any initial activities.  
    * **3.2. Pre-Sales Processes**  
      * The Pre-Sales phase is crucial for setting the foundation for customer success. It involves a series of activities designed to demonstrate the value of CorePlan, align our solution with the prospect's needs, and build a strong business case for adoption.  
      * It is possible for a customer to go through both a desktop study and a POC, or just one, before moving to implementation.  
        * **3.2.1. Solutions Demonstrations**  
          * *Purpose:* The primary goal of a solution demonstration is to showcase CorePlan's capabilities and address the prospect's specific workflow challenges and technical questions. It's about demonstrating how our software can solve their unique problems and deliver tangible value.  
          * *Key Elements:*  
            * **Thorough Preparation**: Before the demo, the Presales team must conduct in-depth research to understand the prospect's industry, business, and specific needs. This includes reviewing their website, industry reports, and any information gathered by the Sales team.  
            * **Customization**: Generic demos rarely resonate. We need to tailor each demonstration to the prospect's specific use case, highlighting the features and functionalities that are most relevant to their workflow.  
            * **Engaging Delivery**: A successful demo is not just about showing the software; it's about telling a story. We need to engage the prospect, connect with their pain points, and demonstrate how CorePlan can make their lives easier.  
            * **Clear Value Proposition**: Throughout the demo, we must emphasize the value that CorePlan delivers. This includes quantifying the benefits, such as increased efficiency, reduced costs, improved accuracy, and enhanced collaboration.  
            * **Handling Objections**: Prospects will often raise objections or concerns during the demo. We need to be prepared to address these confidently and professionally, providing clear and concise answers.  
            * **Call to Action**: Every demo should end with a clear call to action, outlining the next steps in the sales process. This could be scheduling a follow-up meeting, initiating a desktop study, or proposing a proof of concept.  
        * **3.2.2. Desktop Studies**  
          * *Purpose:* A desktop study is a more in-depth evaluation of CorePlan, designed to provide the prospect with a realistic view of how our software would work with their own data and workflows. It allows us to identify potential gaps, assess the feasibility of implementation, and develop a detailed gap analysis document.  
          * *Key Elements:*  
            * **Qualification Criteria**: A desktop study is a significant investment of time and resources, so it's essential to ensure that it's only conducted for qualified prospects. The following criteria must be met before initiating a desktop study:  
              * There needs to be tangible commitment from the potential client.  
              * The SPICED framework needs to be completed.  
              * At least two demos should have been conducted (must include the decision-maker).  
              * If there is a specialized technical requirement, a technical session should have been scheduled to discuss feasibility.  
              * Ballpark pricing has been confirmed.  
              * Demo data cannot adequately address the prospect's pain points.  
              * The Desktop Study Handover document is completed and handed over to the Pre-Sales CS team for configuration.  
            * **Scope and Data Requirements**: To ensure a focused and efficient desktop study, we need to define clear boundaries:  
              * Limit the study to one project.  
              * Include a maximum of five rigs.  
              * Use a maximum of two months of data.  
              * Specify the required data format (e.g., Plod data in Excel or PDF format, charge information).  
            * **Workflow**: The desktop study process should follow a structured workflow:  
              * Introduction: Briefly outline the context of the session and explain the purpose of the desktop study.  
              * Business case presentation: The Sales team presents the business case, highlighting the prospect's needs and the value proposition of CorePlan.  
              * Desktop study demonstration: The CS team conducts a configured demonstration of CorePlan using the prospect's data.  
              * Q\&A: A joint session where Sales and CS address any questions or concerns from the prospect.  
              * Pricing discussion: The Sales team provides pricing information and discusses potential commercial arrangements.  
              * Next steps: The Sales team outlines the next steps in the sales process, such as scheduling a follow-up meeting or proposing a proof of concept.  
            * **Gap Analysis Document**: A key deliverable of the desktop study is a gap analysis document. This document outlines the prospect's needs, details how CorePlan addresses those needs, and identifies any potential gaps or areas that require further discussion or customization. The gap analysis will outline how the findings from the desktop study will inform the business case. This document will be used throughout the sales process and into implementation.  
            * **Data Request Template**: To streamline the data collection process, we use a standardized data request template. This template clearly outlines the required data, format, and delivery method.  
        * **3.2.3. Proof of Concept (POC)**  
          * *Purpose:* A POC is a more formal and in-depth evaluation of CorePlan in a live environment. It's designed to validate that our solution can meet the prospect's specific requirements and provide them with the confidence to move forward with a purchase.  
          * *Key Elements:*  
            * **When to Initiate**: A POC is typically initiated after a desktop study, especially if the study reveals gaps or the prospect needs to test the system in a live environment. However, a POC may also be conducted directly if the opportunity is large or strategically important.  
            * **Criteria for Conducting a POC**:  
              * Strong business case with a clear path to a closed-won deal. The Sales team is responsible for driving the creation of the business case, with support from CS as needed. The gap analysis from the desktop study will be a key input into the business case.  
              * Customer commitment to actively participate in the POC and provide necessary resources.  
              * Alignment with our strategic priorities and resource availability.  
            * **Key Stages and Activities**:  
              * Business case development: Sales and Presales collaborate to help the prospect develop a compelling business case for adopting CorePlan.  
              * Strategic alignment meetings: We conduct meetings with the prospect's champions, stakeholder groups, and our internal teams to ensure everyone is aligned on the POC's objectives, scope, and success criteria.  
              * POC execution: The prospect uses a dedicated CorePlan environment to test the system with their own data and workflows. We provide ongoing support and guidance during this phase.  
            * **POC Alignment Document**: For larger implementations, we create a detailed POC alignment document. This document outlines the POC's objectives, scope, success criteria, roles and responsibilities, timeline, and reporting requirements. This document will incorporate the scope of work, and will be informed by the gap analysis.  
    * **3.3. Implementation Process**  
      * *Objective:* To provide a seamless and efficient onboarding experience for new CorePlan customers, ensuring they can quickly and easily start using our platform to achieve their business goals.  
      * *Key Elements:*  
        * **Scope of Work**: A clearly defined scope of work (SOW) is essential for a successful implementation. The SOW outlines the project's objectives, deliverables, timelines, and responsibilities for both CorePlan and the customer. It serves as a roadmap for the implementation process and helps to manage expectations. The SOW will be informed by the gap analysis.  
        * **Handover Documentation**: The Sales team will provide the Implementation team with comprehensive handover documentation, including the agreed-upon SOW, gap analysis, and any other relevant information gathered during the sales process. This ensures that the Implementation team has all the necessary context to begin the onboarding process.  
        * **Technical Solutions Sense Check**: For enterprise customers, Technical Solutions MUST conduct a sense check of the gap analysis and SOW to validate feasibility and alignment with best practices, prior to the implementation kickoff.  
      * *Key Stages:*  
        * **Kickoff**: The first step is a formal kickoff meeting, where we:  
          * Introduce the implementation team and key stakeholders.  
          * Review the project plan, timeline, and milestones, as outlined in the SOW.  
          * Confirm roles and responsibilities.  
          * Establish communication protocols.  
          * Set expectations for the implementation process.  
        * **Setup and Configuration**: In this stage, we configure CorePlan to meet the customer's specific requirements, as defined in the SOW and gap analysis:  
          * System setup and environment provisioning.  
          * Data migration and integration with existing systems.  
          * Customization of workflows, fields, and reports.  
          * User account creation and permission management.  
        * **Training**: Comprehensive training is essential to ensure customer adoption and success:  
          * Develop a tailored training plan based on user roles and needs, as identified in the gap analysis.  
          * Deliver training sessions (online, in-person, or a combination).  
          * Provide training materials, such as user guides, videos, and FAQs.  
          * Offer ongoing support and resources to reinforce learning.  
        * **Go-Live**: This is the exciting moment when the customer officially starts using CorePlan:  
          * Final system testing and validation.  
          * Cutover from legacy systems.  
          * Ongoing monitoring and support during the initial period.  
          * Celebration of the successful launch\!  
        * **Graduation**: Once the customer is fully operational and achieving their desired outcomes, as defined in the SOW, they "graduate" from the implementation phase:  
          * Transition to the Account Management team for ongoing support and growth.  
          * Conduct a post-implementation review to gather feedback and identify areas for improvement.  
          * Document lessons learned to optimize future implementations.

    * **3.5. Derisking**  
      * *Objective:* To proactively identify and mitigate potential risks throughout the customer journey. We want to minimize any obstacles that could hinder customer success and ensure a smooth, predictable, and positive experience.  
      * *Key Strategies:*  
        * **Proactive Communication**: Open and transparent communication is essential for building trust and preventing misunderstandings. We need to keep customers informed about progress, potential challenges, and any changes that may affect them.  
        * **Early Issue Identification**: We need to be vigilant in identifying potential issues early on, before they escalate. This requires active listening, careful monitoring of customer interactions, and a deep understanding of their business and technical environment.  
        * **Risk Mitigation Plans**: For any identified risk, we need to develop a clear mitigation plan. This plan should outline the steps we will take to address the risk, the resources involved, and the timeline for resolution.  
        * **Escalation Paths**: In some cases, issues may require escalation to more senior management or other teams. We need to have well-defined escalation paths and procedures in place to ensure timely and effective resolution.  
        * **Documentation and Knowledge Sharing**: We need to document all identified risks, mitigation plans, and resolutions in a central repository. This knowledge base will help us learn from past experiences and prevent similar issues from recurring.
