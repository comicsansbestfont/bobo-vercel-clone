# Multi Channel Outbounding

# **The Sequence**

## **Purpose & Philosophy**

This sequence is our primary tool for validating our "Overtime & Overrun" thesis and securing our first 5 paying customers. It is not a blunt instrument; it is a systematic, multi-channel approach designed to surround our prospect with a consistent, valuable message.

**Our guiding principles:**

- **Be Professionally Persistent:** It takes 5-8 touches to earn a conversation. We will be disciplined.
- **Lead with Pain, Not Product:** Every touchpoint is an opportunity to highlight our understanding of their problem.
- **Earn the Right to Their Time:** We will provide value and insight before we ask for a meeting.
- **Sequences are just the Conversation Starters:**¬†The purpose of Sequences is to make an engagement, start a conversation so that we can book a demo.

## **The Strategy**

We will run an 8-touch sequence over 10 business days. This sequence is divided into two "waves" based on the prospect's engagement.

- **WAVE 1 (Days 1-10): The "Overtime & Overrun" Focus.** This is our primary hypothesis. All messaging is 100% focused on solving the pain of uncontrolled employee overtime.
- **WAVE 2 (Days 14-21): The "Subcontractor Verification" Pivot.** It is our test of a secondary hypothesis on a completely disengaged prospect. If they engage with Wave 1 at all, we stay focused on Overtime.

## **Response Handling & Next Steps**

- **If they respond positively at any stage:** Immediately pause the sequence. Reply within 2 hours. Offer 2-3 specific time slots for a 15-minute call and include a calendar link.
- **If they say "Not now, check back in X months":** Pause the sequence. Add a task in HubSpot to re-engage at that time. Add them to a long-term nurture list.
- **After 21 days with no response:** The prospect is moved to a "Closed - No Response" status. They can be added to a quarterly, low-touch marketing newsletter. We will not prospect them again for at least 6 months.

# **Outbound Cold Calling**

## **Guiding Principles for All Calls**

1. **Your Goal is NOT to "Sell":** Your goal is to uncover pain, build credibility, and secure a *specific, committed next step*.
2. **Be a Doctor, Not a Vendor:** We diagnose before we prescribe. Ask questions, listen intently, and use their language back to them.
3. **Aim for Conversation, Not a Monologue:** These are frameworks, not word-for-word scripts. The goal is to sound like a confident expert, not a robot. Be prepared to go off-script and handle objections.
4. **Use Open ended questions** or leading questions over closed ended questions.
5. **Respect Their Time:** Get to the point quickly and professionally. An entire call should last 2-4 minutes.
6. **Always Update the CRM:** After every call, immediately log the outcome, notes, and the next scheduled action in HubSpot. This is non-negotiable.

**Context:** This is our first live conversation, following the initial personalized email. They may or may not have seen the email.

    "How are you currently tracking when your crew is about to go into overtime?"

# **Email Strategy**

## **Introduction - Cut Through the Noise**

Our target prospects‚Äîthe owners and managers of electrical contracting firms‚Äîare on-site, in their truck, or between meetings. They receive dozens of emails a day and have a zero-tolerance policy for generic, time-wasting outreach.

Our mission is to prove, in under 10 seconds, that we are not just another vendor. We are experts in their specific business problem. This guide will teach you the principles and provide the templates to do exactly that.

## **The 7 Principles of Effective Outreach**

*Inspired by best practices and adapted for our ICP.*

1. **Be Short & Simple:** Aim for **50-85 words**. If they can't read it in one glance on their phone, they won't read it at all.
2. **Lead with Their Problem, Not Our Product:** We are selling a solution to "overtime killing my profit," not a timesheet app.
3. **Demonstrate Sharp Research:** Show you understand their current world. A specific project, a recent hire, or a type of work they do proves you're not spamming.
4. **Use Their Language:** Talk about "sparkies," "subbies," and "penalty rates." Speak like an industry insider, not a software salesperson.
5. **Provide Value in Every Touch:** Even if they don't reply, your email should make them think about their business in a new way (e.g., "What *is* the real cost of that overtime?").
6. **Make It Easy to Say Yes:** Our call-to-action (CTA) should be a simple, low-friction question, not a demand for a 30-minute demo.
7. **Create Urgency with Business Context:** Link their problem to real-world events like project deadlines, EOFY, or Fair Work audits.

---

## **Personalization: Be Specific, Not Generic**

**The Anti-Pattern to Avoid:** Every other salesperson is sending this email: *"Hi Steve, congrats on winning the Westfield tender!"* This is lazy personalization. It's a sales tactic, not an insight.

**Our Approach: Connect the Hook to Their Problem** We must connect our research directly to the pain point we solve. This shows we understand the *implications* of their situation.

| **Instead of this (Weak)...** | **Try this (Strong)...** |
| --- | --- |
| "Congrats on winning the Westfield tender." | "Saw you're on the Westfield tender. On a fixed-price job that size, controlling overtime must be top of mind." |
| "I saw you're hiring Project Managers." | "Noticed you're hiring PMs; usually means you're scaling crews and trying to keep labor costs from spiralling." |

**"Cut the Fluff" :**¬†If your research yields nothing unique or feels forced, do not use it. A confident, direct approach is better than weak personalization. Lead directly with the problem.

## **What to Avoid: The 5 Cardinal Sins of Outreach**

1. **The "Brown Nose":** Avoid over-the-top flattery. "I'm so impressed with your company" feels disingenuous. Specifics are better than superlatives.
2. **The "We-We" Problem:** Don't talk about us. "We do this," "Our platform is great." Keep the focus on **them**‚Äîtheir pain, their situation, their goals.
3. **The "Buzzword Bonanza":** Avoid vague terms like "revolutionize," "game-changing," or "streamline." Use clear, direct language that addresses their specific problem.
4. **The "Novel":** Keep emails under 90 words. Busy people don't read long emails; they delete them.
5. **The "Just Checking In":** Every follow-up must provide new value or a new perspective. Never send an empty "just checking in" email.
