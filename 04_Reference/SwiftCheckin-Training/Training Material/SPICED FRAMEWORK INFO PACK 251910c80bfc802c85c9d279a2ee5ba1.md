# What is SPICED?

SPICED is an acronym that stands for:

- **Situation:** The prospect's current operational context and environment.
- **Pain:** The specific challenges, frustrations, and costs they are experiencing.
- **Impact:** The business consequences of these pains and the desired outcomes of solving them.
- **Critical Event:** The urgent reason or deadline driving the need for an immediate solution.
- **Decision:** The process, criteria, and people involved in making a purchase decision.

This framework is designed for a consultative sales approach, which is essential for our success. It helps us act as trusted advisors who diagnose root causes rather than just pitching features4.

| Category | Consultative Sales (Our Goal) | Transactional Sales (Our Old Way) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Approach** | Focuses on building relationships and deeply understanding the customer's unique business needs. | Centers on quick deals with minimal engagement, focusing on product features. |
| **Process** | Involves comprehensive discovery to uncover underlying challenges and long-term goals using a framework like SPICED. | Involves a short sales cycle with scripted conversations and limited needs exploration. |
| **Value** | Built around solving complex business problems, like labor cost overruns or subcontractor invoice disputes, to deliver measurable ROI5. | Often based on direct benefits like feature comparisons without broader business context. |

## The SPICED Discovery Question Framework for Swift Check-in

This section provides tailored questions to guide discovery calls with our initial Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs): Electrical Contractors and Fit-out Contractors.

### **Situation: U**nderstand the prospect's current process for managing crews, projects, and timesheets and payroll

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **Company Details:** Size, number of employees vs. subcontractors, types of projects (e.g., commercial, residential, industrial).
- **Current Tools & Processes:** Software in use (e.g., Simpro, Procore, Fergus, Hammertech) manual systems (paper timesheets, spreadsheets), payroll software (e.g Xero, MYOB).
- **Workflow:** The step-by-step process from scheduling a job to a crew member checking in, tracking hours and overtime, and processing payroll (and/or verifying subcontractor invoices).
- **Stakeholders:** Who is involved in this process (e.g., Business Owner, Operations Manager, Project Manager, Payroll/Admin staff)?

```c
- How do you currently handle the verification and approval of subcontractor invoices?
```

### **Pain: U**ncover the specific financial and emotional frustrations caused by their current situation.

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **Rational Pain (Financial):** Labor cost overruns eroding profit margins, high administrative costs, revenue leakage from inaccurate invoicing, costs of payroll errors.
- **Emotional Pain (Frustration):** Stress from chasing timesheets, disputes with employees or subcontractors over hours, lack of trust in data, dissatisfaction with current tools.

```c
Sample Questions:

- What are the biggest frustrations or bottlenecks in your current timesheet-to-payroll process?

- Have you ever had a project's profitability impacted by unexpected overtime or inaccurate labor costing?

- Can you tell me about a time you had to deal with a dispute over a employee's (or subbie) hours?

- Who on your team feels the pain of these challenges most acutely?
```

### **Impact:** To quantify the negative business consequences of the pain and articulate the positive outcomes of a solution.

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **Negative Impacts:** How inefficiencies affect project budgets, profitability, cash flow, and team morale.
- **Positive Outcomes (Goals):** Desired improvements such as reduced admin time, improved project profitability, better cash flow, and having a single source of truth for labor costs.

### **Critical Event:** To identify OR Create the urgency and "why now" factor that compels a decision.

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **Trigger Events:** A recent major payroll error, a costly invoice dispute, starting a large new project, losing a key admin person who "knows the system."
- **Time Sensitivity:** Upcoming deadlines, end of a financial quarter/year, contract renewals for existing software.
- **Consequences of Inaction:** What happens if they don't solve this problem by a specific date? (e.g., "We'll continue to lose money on projects," "Our admin team is at a breaking point").
    
    ```c
    **Sample Questions:**
    
    - What prompted you to start looking for a better way to manage this now?
    
    - Is there a specific project or deadline on the horizon that makes solving this a priority?
    
    - CWhat are the risks if you continue with your current process for the next six months?
    ```
    

### **Decision**

**Goal:** To map the decision-making process, stakeholders, and evaluation criteria. A key reason for our deals stalling is a lack of clarity on this step11.

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **Decision-Makers:** Identify everyone involved (e.g., Owner, Operations Manager, Finance Manager).
- **Decision Process:** Understand the steps required for approval.
- **Evaluation Criteria:** What standards will they use to judge a solution? (e.g., ease of use for field staff, integration with payroll, ROI).
- **Budget:** Is there an allocated budget, or do we need to build a business case to justify the investment?
