## 1. PRE-CALL RESEARCH (3-5 MINUTES MAX)

<aside>
‚è±Ô∏è

**Time-box this to 5 minutes.** You're looking for ONE strong angle, not writing a biography.

</aside>

**Company Profile:**

- What trade? (Electrical, Roofing, HVAC, Fitout, etc.)
- Company size? (Look for 10-200 employees)
- What services do they offer? (Emergency work? Fixed-price? Subcontractors?)
- Where are they located?

**Key Stakeholders:**

- Owner/Director name
- Operations/Project Manager name
- Office Manager/Admin name

**Quick Hypothesis:**

- What's their likely pain? (Overtime tracking? Payroll chaos? Subcontractor verification?)
- What's their likely awareness stage? (Problem Unaware vs Problem Aware)

---

## 2. WHAT TRIGGERS TO LOOK FOR

### HIGH-VALUE TRIGGERS (Use These First)

**Growth Signals:**

- Hiring new crew/foremen/project managers (LinkedIn, SEEK, Indeed)
- Recently won large contracts (company news, LinkedIn posts)
- Opened new branch/expanded service area (website, Google)

**Operational Signals:**

- Offer 24/7 emergency services (suggests after-hours overtime)
- Fixed-price contracts (suggests tight margins, cost control critical)
- Multiple service lines (suggests complex coordination)
- Heavy use of subcontractors (suggests verification challenges)

**External Events:**

- Recent severe weather in their service area (storms, floods = surge work)
- Regulatory changes (Fair Work compliance pressures)
- Industry busy season approaching (e.g., storm season for roofing)

### MEDIUM-VALUE TRIGGERS (Use If No High-Value)

- Company describes themselves as "family-owned" or "since [year]" (suggests traditional, manual processes)
- Website mentions "100+ projects completed" (suggests high volume)
- Testimonials mention "reliable crews" or "on-time delivery" (suggests they care about operational excellence)

### NO TRIGGER? USE INDUSTRY PATTERN

If you can't find a specific trigger after 5 minutes, use a common industry pain pattern based on their trade.

### ACTION FORMULA

**Permission-Based Close** = "Would you be open to..." (consultative, not pushy)
