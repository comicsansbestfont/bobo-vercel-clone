### Research Requirements

### 1. Company & Product Deep Dive

**Product Analysis:**
- Analyze their website and product positioning
- What problem are they actually solving? (vs. what they claim)
- How clear is their value proposition?
- What's their current messaging approach?
- What's their product differentiation?
- Product maturity indicators (features, polish, documentation)
- Pricing strategy analysis (if visible)

**Target Market Reality Check:**
- Who do they actually seem to be targeting based on their website/marketing?
- Is their stated target market consistent with their positioning?
- How specific/niche is their focus?
- Are there signs of market confusion or trying to serve everyone?

**Current GTM Signals:**
- What marketing channels are they active on? (content, ads, social, etc.)
- Evidence of sales process or CRM
- Lead generation approach (forms, demos, trials, etc.)
- Customer acquisition strategy signals
- Any case studies, testimonials, or social proof?

### 2. Founder Intelligence

**Background & Experience:**
- Founder's professional background (previous roles, companies)
- Relevant domain expertise for this SaaS
- Previous startup experience (successes, failures, exits)
- Technical vs. non-technical background
- Sales/GTM experience level

**Thought Leadership & Presence:**
- LinkedIn activity and content themes
- Other social media presence
- Blog posts, podcasts, interviews
- Speaking engagements
- Community involvement

**Founder Signals:**
- Indicators of coachability (asks questions, shares learnings)
- Ego signals (positive or negative)
- Learning orientation vs. know-it-all
- Openness to feedback based on public interactions
- Network quality (connections, endorsements)

### 3. Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors:**
- Identify 3-5 direct competitors
- For each competitor, document:
    - Their positioning/messaging
    - Target customer
    - Pricing (if available)
    - Key differentiators
    - GTM approach
    - Funding/maturity level
- Why them over their biggest competitor? What's their UVP?
- Why do customers choose them over the competitors? (important)

**Positioning Gaps:**
- Where does this company fit in the competitive landscape?
- What unique positioning could they own?
- Are they blending in or standing out?
- Positioning opportunities they're missing

**Market Positioning Strategy:**
- Category they're competing in
- Whether they should be a category leader, fast follower, or niche player
- Potential blue ocean opportunities

### 4. Market & Industry Analysis

**Market Context:**
- Market size and growth trends for their category
- Recent industry trends affecting their space (positive or negative)
- Regulatory or technology shifts
- Funding environment for this type of SaaS

**Customer Profile Research:**
- Typical customer profile in this vertical
- Customer pain points and buying triggers
- Decision-maker personas
- Typical sales cycle length
- Common objections in this space

**GTM Approaches in This Vertical:**
- How do successful companies in this space typically go to market?
- What channels work best for similar products?
- Typical pricing models and ranges
- Common sales motions (self-serve, sales-assisted, enterprise)

### 5. Business Health Indicators

**Growth Signals:**
- Job postings (team growth)
- Product updates/release notes
- Press mentions or coverage
- Social media growth/engagement
- Customer review sentiment (G2, Capterra, etc.)

**Risk Signals:**
- Stagnant product development
- Negative reviews or complaints
- Founder burnout indicators
- Team instability (LinkedIn departures)
- Funding concerns or runway issues

**Traction Indicators:**
- Customer count (if visible)
- Case studies or logos
- Integration partnerships
- Community size
- Content engagement levels

### 6. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Hypothesis

**Based on your research, develop a hypothesis for their ICP:**
- Company size (employees, revenue)
- Industry/vertical
- Geographic focus
- Tech stack/maturity
- Key pain points this product solves
- Buying triggers
- Decision makers and influencers
- Budget range

### 7. GTM Challenges Diagnosis

**Identify the likely root cause GTM challenges:**
- Positioning clarity issues
- ICP definition problems
- Messaging effectiveness gaps
- Channel-market fit issues
- Sales process/conversion problems
- Pricing strategy concerns
- Founder selling capability gaps

**For each challenge, note:**
- Evidence supporting this diagnosis
- Severity level (high/medium/low)
- Potential impact on growth
- Quick win opportunities

### 8. Comparable Success Stories

**Find 2-3 similar SaaS companies that successfully scaled 0-$1M:**
- Similar space/vertical
- Similar product type
- Australian or similar market
- Document their GTM approach
- Key strategies they used
- Timeline to $1M ARR
- Lessons applicable to this prospect

### 9. Recommended Discussion Framework

**Based on all research, recommend:**
- **Opening Questions** (3-5 questions to quickly diagnose their biggest needs)
- **Key Topics to Explore** (Prioritized list of areas to dig into)
- **Potential Objections** (What concerns might they raise + how to address)
- **Value Demonstration** (Examples/case studies most relevant to them)
- **Red Flags to Watch For** (What would make them not a good fit)

### 10. Quick Win Opportunities

**Identify 2-3 quick wins you could help them achieve:**
- Specific, tactical improvements
- High impact, low effort
- Demonstrable within 30-60 days
- Use these as proof of value

**Special Focus Areas for This Prospect:**
- Since they're pre-revenue with a goal of "first 20-30 customers", focus heavily on:
  - ICP definition frameworks
  - Founder-led sales playbooks for first 10 customers
  - Outbound prospecting strategies
  - Positioning that cuts through AI noise
  - Pricing models for early customers
  - Reference customer acquisition tactics
