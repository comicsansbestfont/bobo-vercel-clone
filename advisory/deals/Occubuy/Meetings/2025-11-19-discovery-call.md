### Partner Model
- 25 partners total across categories
- **50/50 splits** on referral fees (not lead selling like Finder)
- Nurturing leads over 18-36 months, not transactional hand-offs
- Moving to **Qantas-style on-balance sheet model**:

  - Deferred liability covers redemption costs

- Insurers, utility providers
- ~8 property development partners (Mirvac, Meriton mentioned)

## Bank Value Proposition

Nathan identified two key pain points for banks that Occubuy solves:

1. **Customer understanding:** Banks don't understand 20-45 y/o "new to bank, new to credit" customers well enough
2. **High acquisition costs:** Current channels (brokers + trailing commissions) blow their budgeting

Occubuy can send customers directly to bank at much lower cost than traditional channels.

## My Observations

> **Nathan is a natural salesperson** - enthusiastic, narrative-driven, builds rapport quickly. Very likeable. The crypto background gave him community-building skills that translate well.

> **The Amy Sheen Ash hire is a game-changer.** C-suite enterprise access they desperately need for bank partnerships. If she can deliver NAB/HSBC, this could accelerate fast.

> **Bootstrapped discipline is a strength.** They haven't wasted capital on offices or premature scaling. They know what moves the needle.

> **The reluctance to fundraise is both strength and weakness.** Shows integrity and focus on building, but could limit growth at critical moment. Nathan's self-awareness about this is encouraging.

> **Built Rewards comp ($10.7B) is powerful validation** but needs AU market proof. The "dead money" narrative resonates emotionally.

> **Biggest risk:** Long-tail revenue model (18-36 months to property purchase). Trust perception issues also surfacing (Sachee mentioned Perth failed attempt, data harvesting concerns).

> **Biggest opportunity:** Bank partnership play. If they can sign 1-2 AU banks, they have real leverage for international expansion. Amy's access is the key.

> **Personal interest:** Genuinely interested both as advisor and investor. Need to see the deck and understand cap table before committing.
