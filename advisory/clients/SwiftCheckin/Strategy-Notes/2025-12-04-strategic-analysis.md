4. **Team Restructure Complete**
   - Sandy exited (September 2025) - sales philosophy misalignment resolved

### Critical Concerns

1. **Severe Founder-Market Fit Gap**
   - Tech founder (India) selling to Australian construction tradies
   - Both founders (Boney and Nisha, who is his wife) are in India
   - Thick accents - significant barrier for cold calling tradies

   - Relationship-driven industry requires in-person trust building

2. **No Local Presence**
   - Both founders in India
   - Nisha doing outbound from India with accent barrier

   - Events attended (Melbourne Build Expo) - no clear traction
   - Associations explored - no traction yet

3. **SimPro Wedge Not Articulated**
   - 70% of prospects using SimPro with timesheets
   - No clear "why switch" story
   - Competing against "good enough" more than competitors

4. **Unproven Channels**
   - Facebook ads running but ROI unvalidated
   - Non-ICP "referrals" Boney mentioned are unrealistic (knows people in construction but not actual buyers)
   - Only 1 customer to mine for referrals

5. **Cash Runway Critical**
   - December funded only
   - Melbourne Angels: 60-75 day process (won't close in time)
   - No safety net if traction stalls

## 3. Engagement Decision Framework

### Current Situation
- **Paid engagement**: Completed October 2025
- **Sounding board**: Free through January 2026
- **Payment**: Deferred to June 2026
- **Equity proposal**: Friction when discussed
- **Promised**: Weekly catchups through December

### Recommendation: Graceful Disengagement in January

**Rationale:**
1. Already delivered significant value (first customer, positioning shift)
2. What SwiftCheckin needs now is **execution**, not more strategy
3. They need someone embedded 40+ hrs/week driving daily sales activity
4. Founder-market fit concerns mean advisory has diminishing returns
5. Free work sets wrong precedent for practice
6. **The constraints are too severe for remote advisory to overcome**

**Proposed Approach:**
> "I've loved working with you and I'm proud of the progress. The foundations are built - positioning, ICP, frameworks, first customer. What you need now is execution muscle, not more strategy. My highest value is with founders at the discovery stage, and you're past that. If you want me to continue in 2026, I'd need to revisit the advisory + equity structure we discussed. But honestly, I have other founders I'm considering for that model."

This approach:
- Honors existing commitment (through December)
- Leaves door open if they want equity advisory
- Creates optionality without obligation
- Positions disengagement as "graduation" not abandonment

## 6. What Would Need to Be True for Success

### The Only Realistic Paths Forward

**Path 1: Boney Relocates to Australia (Temporarily)**
- 2-3 months in Melbourne doing in-person relationship building
- Learn relationship sales through immersion
- Cost: $10-15K
- Probability of success: Medium (if he commits fully)

**Path 2: Find a Local "Door Opener" Advisor**
- Someone with 20+ years in Australian construction
- Willing to make 10-15 warm intros
- NOT a sales rep (Boney still closes)
- Compensation: 0.5-1% equity or $1-2K/month
- Probability: Low (hard to find, expensive)

**Path 3: Acquire a Local Contractor/Champion**
- Find someone already in the industry who believes in the product
- Commission-only or equity-based arrangement
- They do the relationship building, Boney does product/demos
- Probability: Very Low (why would they work for a 1-customer startup?)

**Path 4: Pivot to Different Market**
- Find a market where remote sales work (e.g., US, where cold calling is more accepted)
- Or find a market segment that's more tech-savvy
- Probability: Unknown

### What Will NOT Work

| Approach | Why It Fails |
|----------|--------------|
| More cold calling from India | Accent barrier, "too busy" gatekeeping, relationship industry |
| Facebook ads at scale | Construction directors don't buy software from ads |
| Non-ICP referrals | People who aren't buyers can't refer buyers effectively |
| Associations/events (remote) | Requires in-person relationship building |
| Channel partnerships | No partner will work with a 1-customer startup |
| Hiring a sales rep | Can't afford base; commission-only won't work at this stage |
