## 7. Stakeholder Discovery Insights

*Key findings from discovery sessions with each team member (July 2025)*

### Sales Process (Sandy)
- Sales process only 3 months old (April 2025 start) - still establishing baseline
- Sandy hired as "sales manager" but operating as founding AE (role misalignment)
- SDR (Kavita) in Chennai with zero construction/SaaS background - needs 3+ months to ramp
- HubSpot under-licensed - missing Sales Hub Pro features, limiting automation
- Manual workflows: Sandy personally enriching contacts, logging calls, managing lists

### Channel Economics (Jayadev)
- Google Ads: $20-25/click making economically unviable for early-stage
- LinkedIn: ICP not tech-savvy enough for standard SaaS CTAs ("free trial" doesn't work)
- Cold calling only viable channel, but 90%+ calls hitting gatekeepers/IVRs
- Reddit showing promise for brand-building (no immediate conversion expected)

### Positioning Evolution (Jayadev)
- Two pivots in 18 months: TAFE contractors → Autodesk integration → Construction timesheets
- Started as "construction management for builders" - lost on feature comparison
- Learned they're actually "labor-focused contractor management for subcontractors"
- ICP refined: Builders (too feature-rich) → Project managers (no resonance) → **Subcontractors 5+ employees**

### Competitive Reality (Nisha)
- Deputy: Generic timesheet software, not construction-specific
- Assignar: Expensive, builder-focused, overkill features, poor UX
- SIMPRO/Fergus/Tradify: Recently identified but not fully analyzed
- SwiftCheckin USP: Construction-specific, subcontractor-focused, affordable, better UX

### Notable Quotes
> "When a builder compared our product to any other product in the market, we lost out on the feature list itself." - Jayadev

> "Our ICP is not tech-savvy. They don't even like a simple thing like a free trial... The Australian market is ten years behind." - Jayadev

> "I was actually bought in to do the ad hoc head of sales thing... But that's not what I initially set out to do completely." - Sandy

## 8. Lessons & Notes

### What Worked Well
- Melbourne in-person trip was critical for relationship building
- Diagnostic micro-workshop format effective (findings + discussion)
- SPICED framework resonated with sales team
- Weekly syncs via Cliq kept momentum
- Stakeholder discovery sessions revealed depth of issues not visible from surface

### What Could Improve
- Could have pushed harder on execution discipline earlier
- More hands-on demo shadowing would have accelerated changes
- Earlier involvement in outbound list building

### Patterns Observed
> Classic "premature scaling" - invested in SDR, Sales Manager, paid ads before proving PMF. Needed to return to founder-led sales.

> "All-in-one platform trap" seen in multiple vertical SaaS plays. Hyper-focus is the only escape.

### Notes for Future Engagements
> Construction/ConTech founders often need permission to focus. They see the whole market and want to capture it all. Strategic constraint feels counterintuitive but is essential at Phase 1a.

## Key Diagnostic Findings (Summary)

### Root Cause
Swift Check-in is a **solution in search of a well-defined problem**. Pre-revenue status is a direct consequence of prematurely investing in scaling GTM before achieving product-market fit.

### Two Validated ICP Profiles

**Profile A: "Overtime & Overrun" Contractor** (RECOMMENDED)
- Mid-sized electrical/plumbing contractors, 20-50 employees
- Pain: Uncontrolled labor cost overruns, fraudulent timesheets
- Value: "Protect project profit by eliminating unplanned overtime"
- ROI: $75K/year savings (5% reduction on $1.5M payroll)

**Profile B: "Subcontractor Verification" Contractor**
- Commercial fitout, subcontractor-heavy GCs
- Pain: Invoice verification for subbies
- Value: "Undeniable proof of work, saving 10-15% on payouts"

### Strategic Imperative
**Pick → Prove → Build**
1. Hyper-focus on one problem, one persona
2. Secure first 5+ paying customers
3. Only then invest in scaling
