The biggest mistake you can make in your go-to-market strategy is _targeting
everyone_.

Instead, start to focus on a specific market segment, a niche.

It‚Äôs a group of customers that share common characteristics, needs, and wants.

It will allow you to:

  * Understand your customer's needs better

  * Tailor your [messaging](https://www.mrrunlocked.com/p/the-ultimate-saas-messaging-framework) to resonate with them

  * Create a product that solves their specific pain points

Once you dominate that segment, you can expand into adjacent ones (Or stay
there forever).

**So what you need to do is to create your ideal customer profile (ICP).**

But an ideal customer profile is more than:

‚ùå company size

‚ùå job title

‚ùå geography

‚ùå hobbies

Instead, your ideal customer profile is a combination of exact information
about:

1Ô∏è‚É£ The company (firmographics)

2Ô∏è‚É£ The persons involved (decision maker & user)

With a powerful ICP, you have everything to create your [sales
messaging](https://startupbusinesstips.substack.com/p/hit-message-market-
fit-b2b-saas-startup) and hit message-market fit.

This means your message resonates with the prospect.

They know it‚Äôs for them.

Your sales messaging in a nutshell is to combine the:

üî∏the exact problem they have (in their own words)

üî∏ the (shitty) status quo (Situation A)

üî∏ the desired dream state (Situation B)

üî∏ their motivation to go from A to B

üî∏ their costs/risk of not going from A to B

üî∏ their motivation to go from A to B

> **Tip: Minimize product surface area at the start**

Here‚Äôs how we did it:

  1. When prioritizing features, **weigh the complexity they add for both your team and the customer.** Don‚Äôt just focus on the initial effort of building and releasing the feature; consider its long-term maintenance and support.
