You can market the exact same product in different ways. That‚Äôs what we call
Positioning.

It‚Äôs a fundamental element of your [GTM

Rob Walling made a very actionable video on the different types of

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Niche down**

Finding an (underserved) segment of the market and building the best tool for
them.

Most of the time it's vertical-specific tools.

Examples: Resource management software for the public sector
([locaboo.com](https://www.locaboo.com/)) or banking account for freelancers
([kontist.com](https://kontist.com/))

**2Ô∏è‚É£ Low-cost player**

Mostly in a more crowded market when market leaders increase their prices (a
lot), you have the chance to build a low-cost version of it.

Best for PLG products.

Examples: Cheaper alternatives to market leaders like Salesforce, Intercom,
Adobe

**3Ô∏è‚É£ Premium-player**

Serve clients that need more human touch (customer service, sales demos...).
You can position yourself as the premium player. Mostly more connected to the
enterprise segment.

**4Ô∏è‚É£ Unique benefit**

You have something very unique that no one else has.

This can be a specific feature or the fact that you serve a specific market
better (e.g. being GDPR compliant, if you sell to DACH market)

Examples: GDPR-compliant alternative to Mailchimp
([www.cleverreach.com](https://www.cleverreach.com/))

**5Ô∏è‚É£ Being the 'lite' version of something complex**

You serve a customer segment that doesn't need the heavy, complex market
leader (e.g. Salesforce or Adobe).

You're building a lightweight version.

Examples: [Pipedrive](https://www.pipedrive.com/) being the lite alternative
to Salesforce

**6Ô∏è‚É£ Mix of the above**

Of course, each of the elements can be mixed. Lite player, niche player and
low cost player and work quite well hand in hand. Also niching down and having
a unique benefit can be very powerful.

Knowing the pain points of your
[ICP](https://startupbusinesstips.substack.com/p/how-to-create-a-powerful-
ideal-customer) is crucial for a [powerful
messaging](https://startupbusinesstips.substack.com/p/hit-message-market-
fit-b2b-saas-startup). You should not only use these pieces of information in
your [sales demos](https://startupbusinesstips.substack.com/p/the-ultimate-
guide-to-sales-product-demos), they should rather be a central element of all
your customer-facing communication - including your website.
