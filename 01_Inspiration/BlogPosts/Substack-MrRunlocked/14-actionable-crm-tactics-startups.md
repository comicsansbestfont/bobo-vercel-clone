When it comes to creating deals for your sales pipeline, I see 2 common
mistakes:

‚ùå Creating a deal too early (basically every prospect)

‚ùå No hard definition/rule for when you open a new deal

If you‚Äôre following a sales-led motion, make sure to only create a new deal

Based on my experience, I see 2 ways that work:

1Ô∏è‚É£ Create a new deal for MQLs (marketing qualified leads) or

2Ô∏è‚É£ only for sales qualified leads (SQL)

MQL and SQL should have a clear definition.

Quick reminder:

MQL = interested in talking to sales (e.g., booked a call with sales)

SQL = MQL becomes SQL if they fit your qualification criteria. I usually
recommend 3 criteria:

‚úÖ ICP Fit

‚úÖ Product-Usecase Fit

‚úÖ Urgency to buy

So make sure that you apply logic when it comes to opening new deals in your
pipeline.

Otherwise, you will dilute the numbers for forecasting.

Every founder should have a weighted pipeline forecast report.

A weighted pipeline gives you clarity on whether you will hit your future
revenue goals.

It multiplies your deal value by the probability of closing the deal.

Example:

  * You have 5 open deals with each 10.000‚Ç¨ deal value

  * All deals are in the stage ‚Äòdiscovery‚Äô with an expected close date next quarter

  * You have a 50% win rate from discovery to won

Based on this information, your weighted pipeline for next quarter is: 25000‚Ç¨.

But let‚Äôs say your actual win rate would be 25%, your weighted pipeline would
only be 12500‚Ç¨.

When I work with founders, I see 3 common mistakes:

‚ùå Not working with deal stage probabilities at all

‚Üí This makes it hard to know if you‚Äôre on track or not.

‚ùå Way too optimistic deal stage probabilities

‚Üí Especially if they are early and don‚Äôt have enough historical data to know
what‚Äôs realistic

‚ùå Not updating probabilities to ‚Äòreal historic‚Äô data

‚Üí Once set in stone, they never update them again.

So my advice:

‚úÖ Review your deal stage probabilities at least once a quarter
