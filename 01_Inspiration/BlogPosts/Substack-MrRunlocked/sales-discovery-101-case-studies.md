There‚Äôs a lot of discussion going on if you should do the discovery and

Rule of thumb: Have discovery and demo in 2 separate meetings if:

‚úÖ selling to mid & enterprise market

‚úÖ selling to multiple stakeholders

‚úÖ higher ACVs

‚úÖ complex products

‚úÖ bigger impact on the operation of the buyer (multiple usecases, relevant

But today, we cover the 101 of your discovery. The fundamentals are relevant,

Let‚Äôs talk about the 4-stage structure of a good discovery.

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Setting the context**

After your small talk in the beginning, it‚Äôs important to manage expectations

make sure you have an aligned view on it.

‚ÄúSo it‚Äôs great to have the call with you today. Before we jump into it, I

  1. We both agree that (on a high-level) X and Y is a good fit and we should discuss next steps.

  2. We both find out, that X and Y is NOT a good fit. And I can recommend you a better fit solution.

  3. We are not sure yet, so we need another session to decide if 1. or 2. is true.‚Äù

Ask if this is aligned and also the expectation of the buyer.

If so, you earned the right to ask questions and learn about their current
situation and potential pain points.

2Ô∏è‚É£ **Discover status quo and pain points**

That‚Äôs the part where you ask relevant questions to understand the current

‚úÖ You qualify the buyer

‚úÖ **your positioning**

4Ô∏è‚É£ **Take decision on next steps**

Now circle back to step 1. Do you and the buyer have enough conviction to take

Case studies are a great social proof. They are one of the core sales &

You use them all over the place:

‚úÖ on your [SaaS homepage](https://www.mrrunlocked.com/p/how-to-high-

‚úÖ in your sales deck

‚úÖ in your outbound & [content activities](https://www.mrrunlocked.com/p/the-

But I see that lots of founders struggle to create great case studies.

**A great case study follows a story.**

Your customers was in a shitty situation A

‚¨áÔ∏è

They faced a lot of challenges. Painful.

‚¨áÔ∏è

They tried a few things. But none of them worked.

‚¨áÔ∏è

Then they introduced your solution.

‚¨áÔ∏è

Pain was gone. They arrived at dream situation B.

‚¨áÔ∏è

And see awesome results.

To make this case study story great.

‚úÖ add metrics (e.g. x% cost reduction, y% more revenue, z% less manual work)

‚úÖ add how they use the product (aka. usecase)

‚úÖ add personal quotes of the customers + image

A great way to get all these information is a 2-step process.

**Step 1: Questionaire shared with client**

‚Äî> Create a document with 5+ questions and share it with the client upfront.

**Questions can include:**

  * What challenges did you face before working with X?

  * Why did you decide to search for a solution? Have you tried other solutions before X?

  * How did you find X?

  * What was your experience with X?

  * What changed since you implemented X in your company?

  * What do you want to achieve with X in the future?

  * Would you recommend X to others? If yes, why and to whom?

**Step 2: (Video-meeting) interview**

If possible, ask for permission to record the interview.

Now you have enough content to create different case study formats - written
case study or also a video case study.
