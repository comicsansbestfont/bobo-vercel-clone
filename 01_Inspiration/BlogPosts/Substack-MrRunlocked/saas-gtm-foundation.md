Let‚Äôs start with what your ICP is.

Your ICP is NOT your Total Addressable Market (TAM).

> **Your TAM = The big market you sell to your investors**
>
> **Your ICP = The part of the market that you target today with your
> product**
>
> **Your ECP = A sub-segment of your ICP, that is easier to win early on.**

It‚Äôs important to understand that your ICP will change over time.

The 'ideal' customers in the 0 to 100k ‚Ç¨ is different than your 'ideal'
customers from 1‚Ç¨ million to 5‚Ç¨ million etc.

So live with the fact that your ICPs will change over time.

Think about it this way: Your ICP today brings you to your next milestone.

Once you hit the next milestone, you can revisit your ICP.

These are your first 10-100 customers. You usually find them via your
[network, referrals, and capturing high
intent](https://www.mrrunlocked.com/i/155000783/tactics-that-i-would-test-in-
to-get-the-first-customers), as part of your ‚ÄúHustle Mode‚Äù.

And they might be different from your ICP, in that they are:

  * Solution aware

  * In the market

  * Experimental

Whereas your ICP might be:

  * not yet solution aware

  * harder to reach

  * Require proof of work

> Again, in your hustle phase, you will most likely end up with different
> customer types and multiple use cases. Not ideal to grow.

So let‚Äôs start with what positioning is.

**So, in a nutshell, positioning means having clarity on:**

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Who** benefits from your product = _Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)  
_ 2Ô∏è‚É£ **What** your product does = _Product Category and/or Primary Use Case  
_ 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Why** it‚Äôs better than the default option = _Limitations of Status Quo
+ Your Key Benefits  
_ 4Ô∏è‚É£ **How** it‚Äôs different from other alternatives = _Unique Differentiator
/ Positioning Angle_

‚Ä¶and being able to **clearly communicate this** across your product,
marketing, and sales touchpoints.

To answer these core questions, you need to ask yourself:

  * Who is your direct & indirect competition?

  * What is your primary competitor (aka. status Quo)?

  * What sets you apart from the status quo (aka. limitations and benefits)?

  * How are you different from your direct competitors (aka. unique differentiator)?

So to help you get tactical on this, let‚Äôs have a look at some frameworks.

One of the best ways to answer ‚ÄúWhat is your product? For what do I use the

  * Usecase

So, you need to take a positioning decision:

**Use Case Positioning vs. Product Category Positioning**

In a nutshell, do this:

‚úÖ **Product Category positioning** : if you‚Äôre building a product in an

‚Üí Good indicator: you will find a category on G2, Capterra, etc., and there‚Äôs

‚úÖ **Use case positioning** : if you‚Äôre building a product in a rather new

‚Üí Good indicator: No product category on G2, Capterra, etc.
